Let's start with what brings them together: in both cases, several people share the same home and choose to live under the same roof. In practice, there are two different systems. Roommates are a lifestyle where several people live together in the same home and share the costs of housing expenses, such as rent and bills.

‍

Coliving, on the other hand, is a more specialized concept of roommate living that focuses on creating a community with a communal lifestyle. Common spaces are often larger and better developed than in a traditional roommate, and there are often organized activities for residents to strengthen the bonds between them.

‍

Coliving can also include services such as shared meals and shared work spaces. Another big difference is that coliving is all-inclusive, making it easy for residents to settle in and get by. More than that, it is already equipped to be conducive to community living: good canapés, a large kitchen, and plenty of dishes.

‍

In short, colocation is more focused on sharing housing costs (with all the management it implies, you have to like math we will not hide it), while coliving aims to create a community and strengthen social ties between residents.
